## Weather variation (critical)
- Unique weather-days: **1,983**; mean inspections/day 70.0, max 125.
- Max within-day weather std = **0.0** → weather is identical for all inspections on a calendar date.
- **Implication: treatment varies only across days; SE MUST be clustered by date (effective N ≈ 1,983 days, not 138,896 inspections).**

## Repeat / panel structure
- Unique establishments (dba+address): **23,737**.
- With ≥2 inspections: 19,611; with ≥5: 11,967; max 59.
- establishment FE feasible: most inspections are in repeatedly-inspected establishments; within-establishment weather variation across inspection dates identifies a weather effect net of fixed location/cuisine/management quality.

## Supported designs
- ✅ **Cross-sectional / pooled OLS-LPM (or logit): fail ~ weather + seasonality + zip + facility/risk FE** — id: weather-on-inspection-day as-good-as-random conditional on season/date; exogeneity of weather to latent establishment conditions. _Diagnostic:_ day-clustered SE; seasonality robustness (month-of-year & DOY controls); placebo with NEXT-period weather.
- ✅ **Establishment fixed-effects LPM: within-estab variation in weather across inspection dates** — id: compares the SAME establishment inspected on hot vs cold/wet days; removes all time-invariant confounders. _Diagnostic:_ two-way (estab + month) FE; cluster by date; test sensitivity to inspection_type composition.
- ✅ **Inspector/route-style heat effect on leniency (violation_count as secondary outcome)** — id: weather affects inspection stringency/throughput. _Diagnostic:_ poisson/NB; same controls; report as secondary.
- ❌ **RDD on a temperature threshold (e.g. freezing 0C)** — id: no policy/behavioral discontinuity at a known temperature cutoff; weather is continuous with no assignment rule. _Diagnostic:_ n/a — REJECTED, no credible running-variable cutoff.
- ❌ **DiD** — id: no discrete treatment turning on at a date for a treated subgroup; weather is continuous and universal. _Diagnostic:_ n/a — REJECTED.

## Recommended primary
Establishment-FE linear probability model of inspection FAIL on inspection-day temperature (and precipitation), with month FE and SE clustered by calendar date; pooled LPM with season/zip/facility controls as the headline if FE sample too thin.
